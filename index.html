About Me

Among the few Malaysian pioneers in AI. Graduated with a BSc in AI in 2014 under the government's prestigious sponsorship program: Ivy League & Ivy League Standard Universities. Technical writing and documentation were necessary for almost every assignment and every project throughout my studies. Every submission must comply with a certain standard: translating complex informatics concepts into clear, accessible documentation for researchers, engineers, and developers.

An ongoing, recurring cybersecurity issue has led me to an extended hiatus from AI programming, repeatedly compromising my devices and rendering them unusable. Without reliable access to functional equipment, programming has not been feasible. While attempting to secure and recover my system amid persistent cybersecurity threats, I lost access to all my files and prior work. This situation has forced me to take on work outside my field of expertise, but those roles have proven unsustainable—technology remains my core strength and area of passion.

My first professional role was as a Celcom dealer in early 2008, without an SPM certificate. As a dealer in tech industry, developing strong understanding of contemporary software and hardware were necessary to effectively communicate product features and benefits to customers and clients.

Prior to this role, I had already begun self-taught front-end development starting in late 2005, and expanded into back-end programming by late 2007—until my growing professional commitments required my full attention. Following this experience, I pursued formal education in the Foundation of Physical Sciences, and subsequently continued my academic journey abroad, specializing in AI.

After graduation, in late 2014, my second professional role was as an AI engineer at an electronics manufacturing factory, assigned by the government to integrate AI into industrial processes. However, at the time, AI was still largely misunderstood and underappreciated within the organization. I was not provided with a computer and access to source code repository. Instead of contributing to technical development, I was often directed to the production floor to observe operators perform their tasks. My role became largely observational. In response, I focused on self-directed research—studying Industrial Revolution 4.0 frameworks and exploring how AI could be effectively applied in smart manufacturing—laying the groundwork for future opportunities in intelligent industrial systems.

Despite ongoing cybersecurity challenges and an extended hiatus from active development, I am now seeking a technical writing role in the AI field, where I can contribute to the clear, accurate, and accessible documentation that drives innovation. My passion remains unchanged: to apply AI in the areas where it is needed most—particularly in empowering individuals with mental and physical disabilities through assistive AI technologies. This is both my mission and vision for the future.
